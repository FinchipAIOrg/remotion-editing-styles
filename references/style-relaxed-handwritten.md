# Relaxed Handwritten Poster

Use warm paper cards, rough handwritten display text, primary-color clashes, and intentional imperfection.

## Editorial behavior

- Cut rhythm: conversational; leave room for personality and pauses.
- Footage: retain natural gestures and imperfect moments when they add credibility.
- B-roll: personal notes, screenshots, sketches, diary-like details, and close observations.
- Transitions: torn-card replacement, quick page flips, underline draws, and restrained wobble.
- Sound: marker strokes, paper flicks, soft taps, or no effect.

## Visual system

- Paper: warm cream `#F7F1DF`.
- Primary: tomato red `#EE1E25`.
- Contrast: sky blue `#16A7F2`.
- Highlight: yellow `#FFD94D`.
- Text: near-black `#1A1A1A`.
- Type: rough handwritten Chinese display face with a highly legible caption fallback.
- Motif: one hand-drawn star, heart, arrow, or underline family.

## Caption system

Keep spoken captions stable. Reserve rotation, rough edges, and oversize treatment for emphasized phrases. Limit rotation to a subtle range.

## Avoid

Polished glass surfaces, perfect alignment everywhere, cold gray palettes, corporate icon sets, and applying rough texture to the entire source footage.
